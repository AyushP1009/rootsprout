Why it matters

Charlotte has food deserts. We're helping change that.

A food desert is a neighborhood where it's hard to find affordable, healthy food nearby. Many families in Charlotte — especially in the west and north corridors — live more than a mile from a grocery store.

RootSprout bridges two gaps at once: teaching families to grow their own food and making it easy to find immediate help when they need it most.

👋 Who We Are

We are Group London — and this is our project.

We're a team of students in PAL Jayden's group, participating in the Emerging Leaders program at our university. We came together around one shared belief: that where you live should never decide whether you eat well.

Charlotte has real food deserts — neighborhoods where families, especially young children, struggle to find fresh and affordable food. We built RootSprout to raise awareness of this issue and give our community real tools to take action.

This site is our Emerging Leaders project, but more than that, it's our commitment to the city we call home.

Our purpose

Why we created RootSprout

Three goals guided every decision we made when building this platform.

📢
Spread awareness
Most people don't realize how many families right here in Charlotte lack access to healthy food. We want to change that — starting with this website.
🗺️
Connect families to help
Awareness isn't enough. We built the Food Locator so that any parent in Charlotte can find a food bank or pantry near them in seconds — no searching required.
🌱
Teach long-term solutions
The Grow Your Own hub teaches families and kids how to grow their own food at home — a skill that lasts a lifetime and doesn't depend on a pantry being nearby.
💡
Lead by example
As Emerging Leaders, we believe young people have a responsibility to use what we learn to make a real difference. This project is us putting that belief into action.
